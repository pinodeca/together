# Foundational Principles

## Purpose
Define mission, values, and decision rules so work can begin immediately without losing direction.

## Vision v0.1
Together is a voluntary global economic community where participants co-own productive assets and
share in income generated by organizations they build and operate.

The long-term aim is to make the majority of people materially better off while expanding meaningful
work, autonomy, and quality time.

## Problem Statement
- Current systems reward concentration of power and wealth, leaving most people with weak bargaining power.
- Technology progress alone has not changed core patterns of insecurity, exploitation, and conflict.
- People need practical pathways to move labor and purchasing power into systems they meaningfully co-own.

## Mission
Build a practical, voluntary, globally cooperative economic model that can be implemented step by step
across jurisdictions and improved through open participation.

## Success Criteria (North Star)
- Material outcomes improve for the bottom 80-90% of income earners relative to local baselines.
- Members gain meaningful ownership, not symbolic ownership.
- Participation remains open, voluntary, and inclusive of part-time contributions.
- The model can be replicated by other economic communities rather than monopolized.

## Non-Negotiable Principles
- Voluntary participation: no coercive membership, no lock-in.
- Meaningful shared ownership: contribution should map to real stake and upside.
- Inclusion by design: part-time, task-based, and project-based pathways are first-class.
- Transparency and auditability: contribution, allocation, and governance rules are inspectable.
- Legal responsibility: local operations must respect jurisdictional law.
- Peace orientation: cooperation across borders is a design goal, not a byproduct.

## Design Hypotheses To Test
- Contribution-time-based ownership can work if multiplier limits are bounded and transparent.
- A capped multiplier (for example, less than 10x) can balance fairness and market incentives.
- Members will shift work and spending behavior if early offerings are credible and useful.
- Layered architecture (ownership OS, operating software, operating companies) can scale globally.

## Decision Rule For v0-v1
When tradeoffs are unclear, prioritize actions that maximize:
- Learning speed
- Member trust
- Revenue realism
- Governance legitimacy
- Replicability across jurisdictions
